How ClearFrame works

ClearFrame removes photobombers from your webcam feed in real time. Capture your empty room once, click each person who is allowed on camera, and anyone else who wanders into frame is erased live - the background behind them is reconstructed so the shot stays clean. Allowed people are remembered by appearance, so they can leave and come back without being erased. No green screen or background blur is required, and the live video pipeline runs in the browser. The optional AI quality audit uploads a matched pair of still frames and four processed-frame crops to Featherless; it does not upload a continuous video.

Why it matters

Video calls happen in dorms, cafes, and shared apartments. Background blur hides your room; virtual backgrounds hide you in a fake room. ClearFrame keeps your real space and just deletes the people who do not belong in the shot.

How it works

  1. Background capture: averages 12 frames of the empty room into a clean plate.
  2. Allowlist selection and tracking: click each person who may appear on camera; a color-histogram and position tracker follows them between frames, and re-identifies them by appearance if they leave the frame and return.
  3. Owner protection mask: a shifted copy of your last-known silhouette prevents the eraser from eating your arm when someone brushes past you.
  4. Removal with edge feathering: everyone else is masked, dilated, and blur-feathered, then filled with the background plate.
  5. Live color correction: a per-channel linear regression matches the stored plate to current exposure, so auto-white-balance shifts do not create a ghost rectangle.
  6. Local relighting: an 8x5 light map interpolates lighting changes (someone flips a switch, clouds move) across the reconstructed region.
  7. Background self-healing: stable non-person pixels slowly refresh the plate, so a moved chair becomes part of the room instead of a permanent smear.
  8. AI quality audit: one click sends the raw frame, the processed frame, and detail crops to a vision model (Qwen 3.6 via Featherless) that hunts for leftover limbs, ghosts, and seams.

All of the vision processing (steps 1-7) runs client-side at interactive framerates on a 640-px pipeline.

Stack

Vanilla JS + Canvas 2D · TensorFlow.js BodyPix (in-browser segmentation) · Vite + Express · Featherless (Qwen 3.6 vision) for the quality audit
