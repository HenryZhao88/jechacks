How ClearFrame works

ClearFrame removes photobombers from your webcam feed in real time. Capture your empty room once, click yourself in the video, and anyone else who wanders into frame is erased live — the background behind them is reconstructed so the shot stays clean. No green screen, no blur, no cloud video processing: everything runs in the browser.

Why it matters

Video calls happen in dorms, cafés, and shared apartments. Background blur hides your room; virtual backgrounds hide you in a fake room. ClearFrame keeps your real space and just deletes the people who don't belong in the shot.

The pipeline

Every frame goes through a pipeline built from scratch on top of BodyPix person segmentation:

  1. Background capture — averages 12 frames of the empty room into a clean plate.
  2. Person selection and tracking — click yourself once; a color-histogram and position tracker follows you between frames, so it keeps you (and only you) even when others overlap.
  3. Owner protection mask — a shifted copy of your last-known silhouette prevents the eraser from eating your arm when someone brushes past you.
  4. Removal with edge feathering — everyone else is masked, dilated, and blur-feathered, then filled with the background plate.
  5. Live color correction — a per-channel linear regression matches the stored plate to current exposure, so auto-white-balance shifts don't create a ghost rectangle.
  6. Local relighting — an 8×5 light map interpolates lighting changes (someone flips a switch, clouds move) across the reconstructed region.
  7. Background self-healing — stable non-person pixels slowly refresh the plate, so a moved chair becomes part of the room instead of a permanent smear.
  8. AI quality audit — one click sends the raw frame, the processed frame, and detail crops to a vision model (Qwen 3.6 via Featherless) that hunts for leftover limbs, ghosts, and seams.

All of the vision processing (steps 1–7) runs client-side at interactive framerates on a 640-px pipeline.

Stack

Vanilla JS + Canvas 2D · TensorFlow.js BodyPix (in-browser segmentation) · Vite + Express · Featherless (Qwen 3.6 vision) for the quality audit
